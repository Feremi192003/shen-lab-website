About

Biography

Jana Shen was born in Shanghai, China, and grew up in several cities before graduating from Nantong High School in Jiangsu Province. After studying at East China University of Science and Technology, she moved to Germany to study chemistry at Bergische Universität Wuppertal.

She received her Diplom-Chemie in 1996. Her diploma research focused on ab initio relativistic calculations of a heavy-metal molecule under the direction of Robert Buenker.

Shen began her doctoral studies at the University of Calgary, where she developed methods based on density functional theory for predicting nuclear magnetic resonance and electron paramagnetic resonance parameters of transition-metal complexes.

She continued her graduate research at the University of Minnesota, working with Darrin York on electronic-structure methods. She earned her PhD in 2003, with dissertation work involving continuum solvent models for semi-empirical quantum calculations.

Following her doctorate, Shen pursued postdoctoral research at The Scripps Research Institute with Charles Brooks III. There, she developed continuous constant-pH molecular dynamics methods for studying proton-coupled molecular processes.

Shen joined the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry at the University of Oklahoma in 2007. In 2012, she moved to the Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences at the University of Maryland School of Pharmacy.

Her laboratory develops and applies molecular simulation and data science tools to investigate biological processes, discover therapeutic strategies, and understand responsive materials.

Research

Scientific Focus

Simulation Methods

Development of computational techniques for modeling protonation, conformational dynamics, and molecular interactions.

Biomedical Discovery

Application of molecular simulation and data science to kinases, proteases, receptors, transporters, and other therapeutically relevant systems.

Drug Design

Computational studies of ligand binding, druggable sites, molecular recognition, and strategies for therapeutic intervention.

Responsive Materials

Investigation of pH-dependent materials and molecular systems with tunable structural and mechanical properties.
