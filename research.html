Research

Following protons through complex molecular systems.

Our research begins with a simple premise: protonation, electrostatics, solvent, and conformation are coupled. We build methods that capture those connections and apply them to biological function, therapeutic design, and responsive materials.

Research directions

From foundational methods to molecular mechanisms.

Each direction is a distinct scientific program, but the same core ideas—ionization, dynamics, and molecular recognition—connect the portfolio.

01

Build the method

Constant-pH molecular dynamics

We develop continuous constant-pH molecular dynamics approaches that allow protonation and conformational states to evolve together in implicit and explicit solvent. The lab’s implementations span GB-Neck2, hybrid-solvent, all-atom PME methods, GPU acceleration, and enhanced sampling in Amber and CHARMM.

02

Measure ionization

pKa calculations

We predict pKa values and protonation states for dynamic proteins, ligands, catalytic residues, and membrane systems. These calculations connect local electrostatics to binding, catalysis, stability, and conformational change.

03

Resolve function

Receptors & transporters

We use pH-aware simulation, enhanced sampling, and machine learning to investigate GPCR recognition, ligand dissociation, proton channels, sodium–proton antiporters, and multidrug efflux pumps.

Design therapeutics

Drug design

We combine molecular simulation, kinetic modeling, quantum descriptors, and machine learning to study inhibitor selectivity, covalent ligandability, kinase dynamics, viral proteases, and opioid–receptor interactions.

Program materials

Materials design
