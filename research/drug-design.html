Molecular discovery

Drug design

Design improves when reactivity, protonation, conformation, and kinetics are modeled together.

Overview

The lab applies molecular simulation and data science to questions in inhibitor selectivity, covalent drug discovery, resistance, and receptor pharmacology. The aim is not simply to score compounds, but to reveal mechanisms that create actionable design rules.

Which molecular features control selectivity, reactivity, resistance, and residence time—and how can those features guide new compounds?

Research program

What we investigate

01

Targeted covalent inhibitors

Predict reactive and ligandable cysteines and quantify the structure–activity relationships of covalent warheads.

02

Kinase conformational landscapes

Model protonation-coupled conformational plasticity and allostery relevant to selective inhibition.

03

Viral proteases

Study catalytic ionization, conformational dynamics, and mutation-driven resistance in coronavirus proteases.

04

AI-enabled profiling

Develop databases and machine-learning models for protein-site ligandability and molecular activity.
