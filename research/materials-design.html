Responsive soft matter

Materials design

Molecular-scale charge regulation can program material-scale structure and function.

Overview

Our materials projects connect pH-dependent molecular interactions to self-assembly, mechanics, and responsiveness. Work on surfactants, chitin, chitosan, and hydrogels is motivated by questions from engineering and industry partners.

How do pH, salt, hydration, acetylation, temperature, and electric fields control assembly and reconfiguration?

Research program

What we investigate

01

Polysaccharide self-assembly

Simulate how chitin and chitosan chains form nanofibrils and polymorphic structures.

02

Responsive hydrogels

Link pH-sensitive crosslinking and chain conformations to reconfigurable material behavior.

03

Electrical programming

Understand how electric fields and electrochemical gradients guide assembly and mesoscale organization.

04

Environmental control

Resolve how salt, temperature, and chemical modification alter flexibility, hydration, and phase behavior.
