Ionization and electrostatics

pKₐ calculations

Protonation states are molecular variables—not preprocessing choices.

Overview

pKₐ values determine which protonation states are populated at a given pH. In proteins and ligands, those values are shifted by solvation, electrostatic interactions, hydrogen bonding, conformational change, and binding. We develop dynamic prediction approaches that account for those couplings.

Which residues and ligands gain or lose protons, when do they do it, and how does that change molecular function?

Research program

What we investigate

01

Protein pKₐ prediction

Calculate residue-specific ionization equilibria while allowing the surrounding protein structure to relax.

02

Ligand microstates

Model binding-induced protonation changes and their effects on water, pose, affinity, and selectivity.

03

Catalytic chemistry

Identify proton donors, nucleophiles, and reactive cysteines in enzyme active sites.

04

Proteome-scale models

Combine curated experimental data with machine learning to accelerate pKₐ and ionization-state prediction.
