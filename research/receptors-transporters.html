Membrane molecular mechanisms

Receptors & transporters

Transport and signaling emerge from coordinated changes in charge, hydration, and conformation.

Overview

Extending CpHMD to transmembrane proteins opened a route to study proton-coupled channels and transporters directly. The lab also investigates how ligands recognize and leave G-protein-coupled receptors, connecting structure and dynamics to binding kinetics and pharmacology.

How do protonation events and ligand interactions drive transport cycles, receptor activation, and dissociation?

Research program

What we investigate

01

Proton and ion transport

Resolve proton-binding sites, charge switches, hydration pathways, and long-range coupling.

02

Conformational cycling

Connect proton release or binding to transitions between functional transporter states.

03

GPCR recognition

Characterize opioid binding modes, receptor microstates, and ligand-specific interactions.

04

Binding kinetics

Use enhanced sampling and machine learning to connect molecular features with association and dissociation behavior.
